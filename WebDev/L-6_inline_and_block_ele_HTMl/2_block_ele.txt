Block-level elemens are those that start on a new line and take up the entire width of their container
by default.They essentially clain all the horizontal space for theselves,pushing any content that comes
after them to new line.

<div>: A generic container used to group elements and apply styling.
<p>: Paragraph element, used for structuring text into paragraphs.
<h1> to <h6>: Heading elements, used to define headings of different levels.
<ul>: Unordered list element.
<ol>: Ordered list element.
<li>: List item element, used within <ul> or <ol> to define individual list items.
<header>: Represents introductory content, typically containing headings and navigation links.
<footer>: Represents footer content, often containing copyright information, contact details, etc.
<section>: Represents a thematic grouping of content within a document.
<article>: Represents an independent piece of content that can be distributed and reused independently.
